Midnight in Manchester

The pavement shines beneath the yellow light,
A lonely traveler whispering to the night.
The cobblestones are wet with silver rain,
And echoes carry down the narrow lane.

A silhouette against the foggy glass,
Watches the heavy, silent hours pass.
No words are spoken, yet the air is deep,
With promises the midnight swore to keep.
— Written May 2026 —

The Paper Weights

Hold down the sheets before the gale begins,
To hide the records of our quiet sins.
Heavy brass resting on an ancient text,
Protecting what we wrote, and what comes next.

For thoughts will fly like leaves upon the breeze,
And lose themselves among the frozen trees,
Unless an anchor binds them to the floor,
Safe behind a locked and heavy study door.
— Written April 2026 —

Echoes of a Quill

A scratch upon the surface, sharp and deep,
A promise that the shadow swore to keep.
The feather bends but does not dare to break,
Recording every calculation that we make.

The iron gall dries to a permanent rust,
Long after our own hands have turned to dust.
The writing stays, a static living sound,
Buried beneath the silence we have found.
— Written March 2026 —
