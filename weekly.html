— June Edition Week 4—

Rain on London Glass

The ink runs dry before the morning breaks,
A dynamic shadow dancing on the wall.
The city sleeps, while lonely memory wakes,
And heavy drops of timeless sorrow fall.

We leave our words on tattered parchment sheets,
Hoping a kindred soul might find the trace,
Of quiet footsteps echoing down the streets,
Lost in the cold English window space.

This week's piece was written during a heavy downpour on the evening of June 24. The rhythmic static of rain hitting the glass pane directly inspired the line structure. I wanted to capture the contrast between the rigid structure of stone walls and the fluid, chaotic nature of ink running wild on damp paper.
