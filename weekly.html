WEEKLY ENTRY • JUNE 2026

Old English Rain

The sky collapses in clean, gray sheets,
drowning out the static of the town.
Ink smears across the parchment like dynamic shadows,
tracing lines that no map can hold.

We watch from deep within the old stone walls,
where ancient drafts whisper secrets through the floorboards,
and write down verses to make the midnight last.

This week's piece was written during a heavy downpour on the evening of June 24. The rhythmic static of rain hitting the glass pane directly inspired the line structure. I wanted to capture the contrast between the rigid structure of stone walls and the fluid, chaotic nature of ink running wild on damp paper.
