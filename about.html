the artist

💡 the origin

Social media is a powerful tool. I was on a journey of finding something new to take part in that was not inclusive of today's lax lifestyle. It showed how different people engaged with pottery and allowed them to create meaningful things, letting their creativity free. So one day, I took a chance and visited a pottery studio in the next town, and it just felt right.

🌀 the connection

I love all forms of art, but I am big on DIY and things that have structure—pottery felt like exactly that. It is a space where I can pour all my emotions. As the wheel spins, it is like a tangled ball of yarn slowly uncoiling and forming a perfect, detangled strand. When trimming is done, it feels like removing everything that was thought to be right but did not quite fit. And glazing is a commitment—a statement that I am finally done and can't change it now.

🏺 the philosophy

Pottery represents absolute commitment. A single piece must go through every single stage, and I stick to it with patience until the very end.
