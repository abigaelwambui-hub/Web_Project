Behind the Wall

The world operates under a harsh modern rule: be strong, show no emotions, and treat feeling deeply as a dangerous vulnerability. But vulnerability is not a flaw—it is where our truth lives.

When I need a sanctuary to drop my armor and just be vulnerable, I go to my books. I have hand-written over eleven personal journals, each page heavy with ink and loaded with precisely how I felt at the exact moment the words touched the paper.

"These journals allow me to relive chapters of my life just as vividly as the day they occurred. They prove that I was at the absolute pinnacle of my emotions."

My words act as an anchor. They don't let me forget; they remind me that I am complex, no matter how simple or flat the outside world tries to define me. Every written line displays a different side of myself when faced with different moments in time, showing a permanent map of my evolution and growth.

I choose to share what I write because, although I may seem like an unyielding stone wall on the outside, I want to show you the backend of my internal world. There are thousands of thoughts running through my mind simultaneously that must escape somehow. By letting mine out, I hope to show you that you can release yours too.
